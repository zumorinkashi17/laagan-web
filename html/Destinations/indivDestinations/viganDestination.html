VIGAN CITY, ILOCOS SUR

Vigan City, located in Ilocos Sur, is a UNESCO World Heritage Site known for its well-preserved Spanish colonial and Asian architecture. The city’s cobblestone streets and historic buildings offer a glimpse into the Philippines' colonial past. Calle Crisologo, the heart of Vigan, is famous for its Spanish-era houses, antique shops, and horse-drawn carriages called kalesas. These heritage houses, many of which have been converted into museums, hotels, and restaurants, showcase the unique blend of Spanish, Chinese, and Filipino influences. Vigan's rich history is further reflected in landmarks such as the Vigan Cathedral, Plaza Salcedo, and the Bantay Bell Tower. The Syquia Mansion, the ancestral home of former President Elpidio Quirino, offers insights into the lifestyle of the Filipino elite during the Spanish period. Another notable site is the Pagburnayan, where visitors can witness traditional pottery making. Vigan is also known for its local cuisine, including the famous empanada and longganisa. The city’s vibrant festivals, such as the Vigan Town Fiesta and the Viva Vigan Binatbatan Festival of the Arts, celebrate its cultural heritage. Walking through Vigan feels like stepping back in time, making it a unique and captivating destination for history enthusiasts and travelers alike.

What to Do:

  • - Stroll along Calle Crisologo and admire the well-preserved Spanish colonial architecture.
  • - Visit the Syquia Mansion Museum to learn about the history of the Filipino elite.
  • - Explore the Bantay Bell Tower for panoramic views of the city.
  • - Try local delicacies like Vigan empanada and longganisa at the Plaza Burgos food stalls.
  • - Watch traditional pottery making at the Pagburnayan.

Travel Tips:

  • - Wear comfortable walking shoes to navigate the cobblestone streets.
  • - Visit early in the morning or late afternoon to avoid the heat and crowds.
  • - Respect local customs and traditions when visiting historical and religious sites.
  • - Bring a camera to capture the picturesque architecture and vibrant street scenes.
  • - Consider hiring a guide to gain deeper insights into Vigan’s rich history and culture.
