CORON, PALAWAN

Coron, located in northern Palawan, is famous for its stunning limestone cliffs, crystal-clear lakes, and vibrant coral reefs. Known for its World War II shipwrecks, Coron offers some of the best wreck diving in the world, attracting divers from across the globe. Kayangan Lake, often dubbed the cleanest lake in the Philippines, is a must-visit with its mesmerizingly clear waters. Twin Lagoons provide a unique experience where fresh and saltwater meet, creating stunning blue hues. Coron Island’s Maquinit Hot Springs, one of the few saltwater hot springs in the world, offers a relaxing experience amid mangrove forests. The town itself is quaint, with a laid-back vibe and friendly locals, providing a perfect base for exploring the surrounding natural wonders. Visitors can embark on island-hopping tours to explore pristine beaches and snorkeling spots teeming with marine life. Mount Tapyas, with its 700+ steps, offers panoramic views of the island and is a popular spot for watching the sunset. The local cuisine, featuring fresh seafood and traditional Filipino dishes, is not to be missed. With its stunning landscapes and underwater treasures, Coron is a paradise for nature lovers and adventure seekers.

What to Do:

  • - Dive into history with World War II shipwreck diving.
  • - Swim in the crystal-clear waters of Kayangan Lake.
  • - Explore the unique Twin Lagoons.
  • - Relax at Maquinit Hot Springs.
  • - Hike up Mount Tapyas for panoramic views.

Travel Tips:

  • - Book diving tours in advance as they can fill up quickly.
  • - Bring sturdy water shoes for exploring rocky shores and underwater activities.
  • - Hire a local guide for island-hopping tours to enhance your experience.
  • - Respect local conservation efforts to preserve the natural beauty.
  • - Pack light, breathable clothing for the tropical climate.
