UNDERGROUND RIVER, PUERTO PRINCESA, PALAWAN

The Puerto Princesa Underground River in Palawan is one of the most remarkable natural wonders of the Philippines and a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Located within the Puerto Princesa Subterranean River National Park, the river runs for about 8.2 kilometers underground, making it one of the longest navigable underground rivers in the world. Visitors can explore the river via guided boat tours that take them through a series of caverns adorned with stunning stalactites and stalagmites. The subterranean river flows directly into the South China Sea, and its lower portion is subject to tidal influences, creating a unique mix of freshwater and saltwater ecosystems. The national park is also home to a diverse range of wildlife, including various species of birds, bats, and reptiles. The lush tropical forest surrounding the area adds to the river's mystique, making it a haven for nature enthusiasts and adventure seekers. The site’s ecological significance and breathtaking beauty have made it a major tourist attraction, drawing visitors from all over the world. Efforts have been made to preserve its pristine condition, including strict regulations on the number of daily visitors. Aside from the river tours, the park offers opportunities for hiking, bird watching, and exploring other nearby natural attractions. The Puerto Princesa Underground River is not only a natural marvel but also a testament to the rich biodiversity and environmental conservation efforts in the Philippines.

What to Do:

  • - Take a guided boat tour through the underground river to marvel at the stunning rock formations.
  • - Explore the surrounding national park on a nature hike to spot wildlife.
  • - Visit the Sabang Mangrove Forest for a paddle boat tour and learn about the local ecosystem.
  • - Relax at Sabang Beach and enjoy the scenic views of the coastline.
  • - Try zip-lining at Ugong Rock Adventures for an exhilarating experience.

Travel Tips:

  • - Book your underground river tour in advance to ensure a spot, as visitor numbers are limited.
  • - Wear comfortable, quick-drying clothes and water-resistant footwear.
  • - Bring insect repellent and sunscreen to protect yourself from insects and the sun.
  • - Carry a waterproof bag to protect your belongings during the boat tour.
  • - Follow all park rules and guidelines to help preserve the natural environment.
